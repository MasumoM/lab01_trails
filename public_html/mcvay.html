McVay Trail

McVay trail is a trail that follows the bed of the former Chicago, Burlington and Quincy railroad and it is flanked by trees. It was named for Ira B. McVay who was the first landowner of the railroad right-of-way that he purchased for the United States Government in 1851

TRAIL FACTS

McVay provides a one poit six(1.6) mile long paved route alowing many diferent activities and uses. The trail crosses the treet at 15th Street heading south to E. 2nd Anenue/route 92 from that point it heads east to Pickard Park. The trail has a number of places to rest as well as parking which is available at Pickard Park, 1468 Hwy 92 East and at the N. 5th Street terminus.
